# ISIC-2024 SLICE-3D — Exploratory Data Analysis

Single-dataset, no-external-data study of the ISIC-2024 SLICE-3D skin-lesion task.
All figures below are generated by `reports/eda/make_eda.py` (read-only on `data/`, seed 42,
dpi 150, Okabe-Ito colorblind-safe palette). This page is the EDA for the companion website.

---

## Headline dataset facts

| Fact | Value |
|---|---|
| Lesion crops (rows) | **401,059** |
| Malignant lesions | **393** |
| Benign lesions | **400,666** |
| Prevalence | **0.098%** (≈ 1 malignant per 1,021 crops) |
| Unique patients | **1,042** |
| Patients with ≥1 malignant lesion | **259** (24.9%) |
| Columns | 55 (35 float, 18 string, 2 int) |
| Real (non-leak) feature columns | 36 |
| Imaging | every crop is a *3D Total-Body-Photography* tile (`TBP tile: close-up`), 2 tile subtypes (`3D: XP`, `3D: white`) |
| Source institutions (attribution) | 7 (MSKCC, Hospital Clínic de Barcelona, Univ. Basel, Frazer Institute/UQ, ACEMID MIA, MedUni Vienna, Univ. Athens) |

**Extreme class imbalance is the defining property of this task** — hence the official metric is
**partial AUC above 80% TPR** (range [0, 0.20]), not plain AUC.

### Diagnosis / leak columns (train-only — NOT predictors)
The following columns are present only in train and encode the answer or post-biopsy pathology.
They must never be fed to a model: `iddx_full`, `iddx_1`..`iddx_5`, `mel_mitotic_index`,
`mel_thick_mm`, `lesion_id`, `tbp_lv_dnn_lesion_confidence`. (`iddx_1` is literally
Benign / Malignant / Indeterminate.) They are used here for EDA framing only.

### Missingness (real feature columns; all others are 100% complete)
| Column | Missing |
|---|---|
| `sex` | 2.87% |
| `anatom_site_general` | 1.44% |
| `age_approx` | 0.70% |

### Malignant rate by body site
| Site | n | Malignant rate |
|---|---|---|
| head/neck | 12,046 | **0.648%** |
| anterior torso | 87,770 | 0.093% |
| posterior torso | 121,902 | 0.084% |
| upper extremity | 70,557 | 0.081% |
| lower extremity | 103,028 | 0.071% |

Head/neck carries **~7× the baseline malignant rate** of any other site despite being the smallest site.

### Malignant rate by sex
| Sex | n | Malignant rate |
|---|---|---|
| male | 265,546 | 0.103% |
| female | 123,996 | 0.088% |

Males are over-represented (68% of sexed crops) and slightly more likely malignant.

### Within-patient (ugly-duckling) statistics
- **392 of 393** malignant lesions come from a patient who *also* has benign lesions, so almost every
  positive can be judged relative to that patient's own "normal" moles.
- On `clin_size_long_diam_mm` the malignant lesion sits at a **median 88th within-patient percentile**;
  **45.7%** of malignant lesions are in the **top 10%** of their own patient's lesion sizes.
- This is the quantitative basis for the engineered "ugly-duckling" deviation features.

*1,042 patients with a heavily right-skewed lesion count (median 241, max 9,184); the 259 patients

reinforcing that splits must be patient-grouped to avoid leakage.*

### Fig 3 — Demographics
![Demographics](fig3_demographics.png)

*Malignant lesions skew toward older ages (peak ~60–75 vs a broader benign mass), males contribute

### Fig 4 — Lesion size
![Lesion size](fig4_lesion_size.png)

*On both clinical longest diameter and TBP area, the malignant distribution is shifted ~2× larger
than benign (medians annotated) — raw lesion size is a strong, cheap univariate signal.*

### Fig 5 — Color & border "ugly-duckling" signals
![Color and border](fig5_color_border.png)

*Hue angle (`tbp_lv_H`) separates the classes most cleanly (univariate AUC 0.81); lesion–skin
contrast, border/color irregularity and eccentricity all shift toward higher values for malignant,
confirming the TBP color/geometry features carry real, independent signal.*

### Fig 6 — Correlation of key TBP features
![Correlation](fig6_correlation.png)

*The 21 core geometry/color features form tight blocks (size group: diameter/area/perimeter/minor-axis;
color group: A/B/deltaA/deltaB), so the GBDT sees substantial redundancy — useful for robustness but
a sign that a handful of axes capture most of the variance.*

### Fig 7 — Sample lesion crops
![Sample crops](fig7_sample_crops.png)

*Eight malignant (top, orange border) vs eight benign (bottom, green border) crops: malignant lesions
tend to be larger, darker and more color-varied, but the visual overlap with benign is large — which is
exactly why a learned image expert is needed on top of tabular features.*

### Fig 8 — Image-embedding class separation
![Embedding t-SNE](fig8_embedding_tsne.png)

*PCA-50 → t-SNE of the ConvNeXtV2-nano OOF embeddings (all 393 positives + 3,000 random negatives):
the malignant points concentrate into a recognisable region of feature space rather than scattering
uniformly, evidence that even a small CNN learns a malignancy-relevant representation.*

### Fig 9 — Ugly-duckling illustration
![Ugly duckling](fig9_ugly_duckling.png)

*For three patients with one malignant lesion among many benign ones, the malignant lesion sits at the
top of its own patient's size distribution (100th, 100th, 98th percentile) — a concrete picture of the
ugly-duckling sign that within-patient deviation features encode.*

## Most striking takeaways

1. **The imbalance is the whole problem.** 0.098% prevalence (393 positives in 401k crops) is why the
   task uses partial-AUC-above-80%-TPR and why patient-grouped, target-stratified CV is non-negotiable —
   the folds confirm a clean 77–83 positives each with zero patient straddling folds.

2. **Tabular signal is real and cheap.** Single features already separate well: hue `tbp_lv_H` reaches
   univariate AUC 0.81, and lesion size (diameter/area) shows a clean ~2× malignant shift. This is the
   evidence base for the LightGBM-first architecture.

3. **The ugly-duckling sign is quantitatively present.** 392/393 malignant lesions live alongside the
   same patient's benign moles; the malignant one sits at a median 88th within-patient size percentile,
   with 46% in the patient's top 10%. Within-patient deviation features are well-motivated, not folklore.

4. **The small CNN adds an orthogonal axis.** ConvNeXtV2-nano embeddings cluster the 393 positives into
   a distinct region of feature space despite heavy benign/malignant visual overlap in raw crops —
   justifying stacking an image OOF probability into the GBDT, while head/neck's ~7× elevated rate
   flags a site prior the model should respect.
