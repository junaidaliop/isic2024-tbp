Credits & Positioning

Gracious credit to the leaderboard champions and the public-notebook lineage we learned from

This project stands on the shoulders of the ISIC-2024 community. We are explicit about what we reused versus what is ours, and we credit the people whose work taught us the task.

The leaderboard champions

We did not beat the unconstrained private leaderboard, and we are not trying to — we banned the two ingredients (external data, synthetic positives) that the top teams relied on. Their results are the benchmark our constrained claim is positioned against, and they deserve full credit.

Note1st place — Ilya Novoselskiy

Private pAUC 0.17264 (post-competition reports cite ~0.1755 for the best unconstrained configuration). An ensemble of EVA-02 + EdgeNeXt image models fused with a GBDT tabular stack. Crucially, the solution used external ISIC-archive dermoscopy data and ~30,000 Stable-Diffusion-synthesized malignant lesions — both of which we deliberately ban.

The most instructive detail for our study: their own ablation reports the ~30k synthetic lesions added only +0.0007 pAUC. On a 393-positive task, fabricating 30k positives bought almost nothing — a sobering data point for the “just add synthetic data” reflex, and part of why we judged the label-validity risk not worth taking.

Note2nd & 3rd place
  • 2nd — uchiyama33 — strong image + tabular ensemble; a source of the EMA + mixup recipe we adopted for the small backbone.
  • 3rd — kyohei-123 — likewise a top image/tabular blend.

Both are credited for the public methodology that shaped the field’s image-expert recipe.

The public-notebook lineage

Our tabular feature engineering and overall recipe descend directly from the public Kaggle notebooks. We learned the task from them and reuse their ideas with attribution:

Source What we learned / reused
greysky — “only tabular” The LightGBM hyperparameters and the tabular-first thesis. Our lgb_bag params are greysky-lineage; the bagging + undersampling structure follows this work.
snnclsr Tabular feature-engineering patterns and CV structure.
motono0223 Image-baseline training recipe (small backbones, undersampling).
andreasbis — “CNN preds as features” The core stacking idea: feed an image model’s OOF probability into the GBDT as a feature.
richolson Additional baseline ideas and ensembling practice.

We also vendored the official ISIC-2024 scorer (src/metric_official.py, © 2024 N. R. Kurtansky, MSKCC) verbatim and attributed, purely to prove our metric matches it.

What we reused vs. what is ours

  • The CNN-preds-as-a-feature stacking idea (andreasbis).

ImportantOur own contributions
  1. The efficiency frontier as the deliverable. We reframe the task from “one private-LB number” to a quality-vs-cost Pareto frontier across params, FLOPs, and CPU latency — so a model only counts if it earns its cost. Every reported model is one measured point.
  2. The no-external / no-synthetic discipline, made rigorous. A locked scope (docs/DECISIONS.md), a cv-guardian with veto power, and a hard no-leak guarantee — turning “we didn’t cheat” into something audited, not asserted.
  3. The image-space ugly-duckling. Extending the tabular ugly-duckling idea into the image expert (a within-patient image-deviation signal) — tried, measured, and reported even though it did not help, because honest negatives are the point.
  4. A complete, honest negative-results catalogue. Meta-stacker, learned MoE gate, image embeddings, heavy transformers, over-strong EMA — every one logged with its number. The finding that complexity consistently lost at 393 positives is itself the contribution.

Where our claim sits

WarningHonest positioning
  • We claim SOTA within the no-external-data / no-synthetic / single-dataset class, on a transparent efficiency frontier with leak-audited CV.
  • We do not claim to beat the champions’ unconstrained ~0.1755 — that used the very ingredients we banned, and matching it is impossible by construction.
  • Our best constrained point is OOF pAUC 0.17430 at 15.8 M params / 2.46 GFLOPs / 61 ms CPU. The honest private-test projection is that minus ~0.01–0.02 (see Results).
